Introduction

Agarwood inoculation products and services in Nepal Agarwood production is a natural and complex process that is initiated when an Aquilaria tree contracts a fungal infection. Being a natural process, it is never certain whether an Aquiralia tree is going to produce agarwood or a commercially valuable quantity of the same or not. Therefore, in order to ensure the success of agarwood plantations and commercial production of the highly valuable non-timber resinous wood, researchers have invented a highly reliable technique called agarwood inoculation.

Agarwood inoculation is an artificial inducement technique used to induce agarwood production in young Aquiralia trees. The inoculation process includes the induction of artificial wounds in the tree’s trunks followed by the introduction of a stimulant agent into the injury induced. The inducement technique is a highly reliable and effective method that ensures higher amounts of agarwood production.

A Brief about Agarwood Plantation

Short history of plantation:

Agarwood is a black, aromatic resin found imbedded in the usually pale wood of Aquilaria species. Agarwood production is associated with the wounding and fungal growth of Aquilaria trees. Agarwood is a highly precious product that has been traded for hundreds of years in various regions of the world. The plantation is extensively dispersed throughout South and Southeast Asia.

The economic value

The international commerce of agarwood includes wood, wood chips, powder, oil, etc. Aquiralia is the most precious tree in the world, with significant domestic and worldwide markets. Depending on the quality of the agarwood, its economic worth can range from $100,000 per kilogram for outstanding pure wood material to $100 per kilograms for inferior quality. The present global market for oud oil and associated agarwood products is believed to be worth $6–$8 billion.

The Medicinal Values:

Different species of Aquiralia possess different medicinal properties. Some of the common ones are: it is used to soothe muscle spasms, reduce fevers, and treat rheumatism, smallpox, and stomach aches. Moreover, it can help with worm infestation, wound healing, treatment of colds, coughs, and asthma, and may protect against both osteoarthritis and rheumatoid arthritis, as well as treat liver cirrhosis and renal issues and cure jaundice.

A Brief about Sandalwood Plantation

Short history of plantation:

P. santalinus, often known as Red Sandalwood, is a native species unique to India, only discovered in the southern Eastern Ghats. The white sandalwood, an evergreen tree, is indigenous to southern India and Southeast Asia. It is primarily found in the Indian states of Karnataka and Tamil Nadu.

The economic values:

One tonne of red sandalwood is sold in the international market at a price range of $70,000 to $1,15,000.

The Medicinal values:

Red sandalwood is highly priced due to its medicinal properties, as it has several therapeutic benefits and is utilised in alternative medicine. White sandalwood oil and powder are used in the treatment of skin diseases like acne and eczema, and respiratory illnesses such as the common cold, bronchitis, and sore throat. Moreover, it aids in the treatment of UTIs, cardiovascular illnesses, and liver and gallbladder issues.
